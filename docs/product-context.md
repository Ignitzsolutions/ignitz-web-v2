# Ignitz Product Context

## Company and offer

Ignitz is an enterprise AI operating partner. The website presents three connected offers: corporate AI training, AI product development, and embedded expert teams. Supporting areas include AI products, incubator projects, hackathons, insights, careers, and company information.

## Primary audience

Senior enterprise buyers evaluating a partner for AI capability building and delivery: transformation leaders, technology executives, learning leaders, and business owners with operational AI initiatives.

## Primary website outcome

Help a qualified buyer quickly understand the relevant Ignitz capability, see credible proof and operating method, and begin a focused commercial conversation.

## Current commercial journeys

- Understand the company from the homepage, choose a capability, and contact Ignitz.
- Evaluate training options and request a consultation.
- Evaluate custom AI product development and request a project estimate.
- Evaluate embedded expert teams and discuss a delivery need.
- Review products, incubator work, hackathons, blogs, team, and careers as supporting evidence.

## Brand constraints

- Premium, editorial, high-trust, and commercially serious.
- Light theme only.
- Ignitz navy and orange are the principal accents.
- Interfaces and motion must clarify business meaning.
- Avoid generic SaaS card grids, decorative gradients, novelty graphics, and unsupported claims.

## Content and system constraints

- Reusable content stays in typed files under `src/content` until a CMS is added.
- Sanity-backed collections must tolerate missing configuration or content.
- Placeholder customer proof, metrics, testimonials, permissions, and legal content must remain isolated until approved.
- Existing business logic and API behavior must be preserved during visual work.

## Inputs still needed from stakeholders

- Approved customer logos and usage permissions.
- Approved testimonials, case-study metrics, and supporting evidence.
- Final legal copy for privacy, terms, and accessibility pages.
- Production contact and quote-form delivery endpoints and ownership.
- Confirmation of the three highest-value buyer workflows and their conversion targets.
